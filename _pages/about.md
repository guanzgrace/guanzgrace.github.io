## Organ Allocation Research

My PhD research was on **market design (matching)**: Improving the complex kidney allocation system with **ML, optimization, simulation, and experimentation** and had real-world policy impact. Highlights include:

- Developed heuristics [1] and end-to-end ML models [2, 3] to predict nonuse risk of deceased donor kidneys, developing scalable pipelines and evaluation workflows (Python)
- Reached out directly to C-suite leaders at over 15 organ procurement organizations (~25% of the nation's total) and recruited them to a **pilot switchback experiment** [4], applying causal inference methods to measure the causal effect of integrating ML predictions into clinical workflows (Python)
- Developed a scalable, optimized kidney allocation **simulator** that modeled real-world acceptance behavior and competing risks, and leveraged market design principles to design, optimize, and test new allocation policies with the simulator [5] (Python, Pytorch, C, C++)
